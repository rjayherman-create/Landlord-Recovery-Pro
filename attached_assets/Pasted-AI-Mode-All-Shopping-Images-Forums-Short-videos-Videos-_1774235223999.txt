Filing a property tax grievance yourself is entirely possible in New York, and doing so can save you the typical 
50% commission of the first year's savings charged by firms like Maidenbaum. While there isn't a single "TurboTax-style" private software for this, most counties have established digital portals that automate the submission process for you.

Where and How to File Yourself
The process and tools vary depending on your location, but the core requirement is proving your home's market value is lower than the town's assessment.

Nassau County: Use the Assessment Review on the Web (AROW) portal. It is highly automated, allowing you to file an AR-1 form online and use their built-in Sales Locator tool to find comparable property sales (comps) to support your case.

Suffolk County: Filing is done at the town level (e.g., 
Islip

). You must file Form RP-524 with your specific Town Assessor.

New York City: Residents must use the NYC Tax Commission portal for Class One properties.

Westchester & Upstate: Most municipalities follow the state-standard Form RP-524, typically filed by the "Grievance Day" (often the 4th Tuesday in May).

Can You Make Money Doing This?
Yes, you can potentially turn this into a business or side income, but there are legal boundaries to consider:

Acting as a Representative: New York law allows an "aggrieved party" to designate a representative to file on their behalf. Some individuals charge a flat fee (e.g., $399) instead of a percentage commission to remain competitive with larger firms.

Legal Restrictions: While you do not need to be a lawyer to file a grievance or attend a Board of Assessment Review (BAR) hearing, you cannot practice law without a license. Representing others in a Small Claims Assessment Review (SCAR) or higher court may require specific authorization or legal standing.

Automation Savings: By using automation (like scraping public records for comps), you can lower your overhead, allowing you to charge less than the standard 50% "success fee" used by major competitors.
